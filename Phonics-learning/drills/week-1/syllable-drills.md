## Day 1 — VCCV Syllable Split Rule

### The Rule

> **VCCV:** When two consonants sit between two vowels, **split between the consonants**.

```
V  C  C  V
   ↑ split here
```

**Steps:**
1. Find the two consonants between vowels.
2. Split between them.
3. Pronounce each syllable, then blend.

### Part B: Flexible VCV Rule (3–4 min)

> **VCV:** One consonant between two vowels — split **after** the consonant (closed) **or before** it (open).

| Type | Pattern | Vowel sound | Example |
|------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Closed** | VC \| V | Short vowel in 1st syllable | ro-bot, cab-in, lev-el |
| **Open** | V \| CV | Long vowel in 1st syllable | ba-by, ti-ger, ho-tel |

**Decision tip:** If the first syllable sounds short, split after the consonant. If it sounds long, split before.

## After the Drill

Move to **reading aloud** (7–10 min). When you hit an unfamiliar word:
1. Identify VCCV or VCV pattern.
2. Split and pronounce each syllable.
3. Blend and continue reading.
